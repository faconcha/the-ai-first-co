# Daniel Priestley - Marketing & Business Principles

## Oversubscribed Method
A business becomes oversubscribed when demand exceeds supply. Steps:
1. Campaign-driven approach: launch campaigns, don't just sell continuously
2. Signal demand before supply: gauge interest before committing resources
3. Create a waiting list or application process
4. Release in limited batches
5. Build a market ecosystem around your product, not just a sales funnel

## The 7P Score (Personal Brand Strength)
Rate 1-10 on each to assess market positioning:
1. Pitching: Can you clearly articulate your value in 60 seconds?
2. Publishing: Are you consistently creating valuable content?
3. Product: Is your product/service remarkable and referable?
4. Profile: Does your public profile demonstrate authority?
5. Partnerships: Do you have strategic alliances that extend reach?
6. PR & Publicity: Are credible third parties talking about you?
7. Performance data: Do you track and optimize based on numbers?

## KPI Method (Key Person of Influence)
Five-step path to becoming the go-to person in your industry:
1. Perfect your Pitch: become known for something specific
2. Publish your ideas: books, articles, videos, podcasts
3. Productize your value: make it scalable and repeatable
4. Build a Profile: establish credibility and visibility
5. Form strategic Partnerships: collaborate with complementary leaders

## Campaign-Driven Sales
- Warming up: signal what's coming, build anticipation (2-4 weeks)
- Informing: educate the market on the problem and your approach (1-2 weeks)
- Launch: open for business with scarcity and social proof (1 week)
- Overdelivering: exceed expectations to generate referrals (ongoing)
- Reviewing: measure results, gather testimonials (1 week)

## Ascending Transaction Model
Guide customers through increasing levels of commitment:
1. Content (free): articles, social posts, videos that demonstrate expertise
2. Products (low-cost): books, templates, small tools
3. Services (medium): group programs, workshops, consulting
4. Enterprise (high): custom solutions, retained partnerships

## Ecosystem vs. Sales Funnel
- Traditional funnel: awareness → interest → decision → action (linear, lossy)
- Ecosystem approach: create a world around your brand where every touchpoint adds value
- Use events, communities, content, and products that all reinforce each other
- People should encounter your brand from multiple angles before buying
